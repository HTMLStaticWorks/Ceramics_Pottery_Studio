Our History

A legacy formed in clay

Est. 2009

The First Spark

What started as a modest workshop in a quiet corner of the artisan district grew from a simple realization: that modern speed has detached us from our tactile nature.

Our founder Sarah Vost opened the studio with three kick-wheels and a dream to preserve slow craft in a fast world. Since 2009, we've remained faithful to that initial vision.

Our Ethos

The Evolution of Thought

"We didn't just want to build pots; we wanted to build the patience required to make them."

In the early years, our focus was purely on technical survival—firing the kiln, keeping the wheels spinning. As our collective grew, our philosophy shifted toward the psychological benefit of the medium.

Pottery became a radical act of presence. Today, we stand as advocates for the 'slow art' movement, proving that quality of craft directly mirrors quality of life.

Clay&Craft Today

We are now a global community of practitioners, connected by a shared love for the tactile. Our studios utilize carbon-offset kilns and recycled clay programs, merging ancient practice with modern responsibility.

Our modern programs include advanced chemistry Masterclasses and inclusive mentorships, ensuring the craft remains vibrant for the next generation.
